Health Information

Diabetes Mellitus (DM)

Diabetes is a chronic disease that occurs when the pancreas is no longer able to make insulin, or when the body cannot make good use of the insulin it produces.

Symptoms:

  • Increased thirst and hunger
  • Frequent urination
  • Fatigue
  • Blurred vision
  • Slow-healing wounds

Prevention & Control:

  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Eat a balanced diet low in sugar and refined carbs
  • Exercise regularly
  • Monitor blood sugar levels
  • Take prescribed medications as directed

Hypertension (HTN)

Hypertension, or high blood pressure, is a condition in which the force of the blood against the artery walls is too high.

Symptoms:

  • Often no symptoms (silent killer)
  • Headaches
  • Shortness of breath
  • Nosebleeds
  • Dizziness

Prevention & Control:

  • Reduce salt intake
  • Maintain a healthy weight
  • Exercise regularly
  • Limit alcohol consumption
  • Take prescribed medications consistently

Hepatitis B (HBV)

Hepatitis B is a viral infection that attacks the liver and can cause both acute and chronic disease.

Symptoms:

  • Fatigue
  • Jaundice (yellowing of skin and eyes)
  • Abdominal pain
  • Loss of appetite
  • Nausea and vomiting

Prevention & Control:

  • Get vaccinated
  • Practice safe sex
  • Avoid sharing needles or personal items
  • Ensure safe blood transfusions
  • Regular medical check-ups if infected

Other Common Diseases

Malaria

Prevention: Use mosquito nets, eliminate standing water, take prophylactic medication when traveling to endemic areas.

HIV/AIDS

Prevention: Practice safe sex, use sterile needles, get tested regularly.

Respiratory Infections

Prevention: Wash hands frequently, cover mouth when coughing, get vaccinated against flu and pneumonia.
